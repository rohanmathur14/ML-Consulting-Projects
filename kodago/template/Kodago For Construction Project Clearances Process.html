General Process To Get Clearance

For any site to get constructed peacefully, it requires getting all the allowances and sanctions from the respective authorities. Any commencement of the project without these mentional lists of approvals invite huge penalties and sometimes stay in order.

One of the most common approvals is "Environmental Clearance". There are certain steps that are to be followed to get the clearance.

  • The project proponent adheres to the existing siting guidelines before the commencement of the proposed plan which is followed by the EIA report.
  • After the EIA report is ready and is approved by the state pollution control board (SPCB), it issues the NOC, i.e. the consent to establish the property.
  • SPCB then arranges the public hearing to hear the objections/suggestions from the public.

All these documents consist of images of the approved land, videos of public hearing, documents related to construction project, google map coordinates of the proposed land, information consisting of clearance status, and much more.

It is a tedious task for the construction company to manage diverse types of information all together under one file. This invites chaos within the workflow.

All the documents along with images, map coordinates, and videos are then aggregated together to be submitted to the environmental appraisal committee.

Once the assessment and evaluation of the project report is completed, the clearance is finally granted to the project proponent.
