Industrial Engineering- The Way Towards Revolution

Talking about the industrial revolution that started in the 18th century, when people just started gaining ground on what industrial processes are and how they can revolutionize the lifestyle of the people, many people took the initiative to step into the world of industries.
In the early stage of the revolution, industrial engineering was only restricted to the process to enhance the profitability and efficiency of the manufacturing operations. Over time, engineers were served various challenges that arose due to inventions of new machinery and processes to optimize it for maximum productivity.

This is when the concept of industrial engineering was introduced to the rest of the world. Let us traverse through this blog to know about this course of engineering in detail along with its specifications and future scope.

What Is Industrial Engineering?

Industrial engineering is the study that focuses on the development, implementation, improvement, and evaluation of the integrated systems of manpower, information, capital, knowledge, and equipment. This engineering division is mainly concerned with the optimization of the complexities within the system, process, or organization.
It is the field of engineering that uses specialized knowledge and skills along with the principles of engineering to specify and evaluate the output results from the systems. It even eliminates the waste of money, time, resources, materials, and energy.
Depending upon the motives of the user, industrial engineering can be renamed production engineering, manufacturing engineering, or operation engineering.

What Does An Industrial Engineer Do?

Industrial engineers play an important role in all the stages of production, management, and processing. Here is the list of the job responsibilities of industrial engineers.

  • Industrial engineers may have to design a new facility to optimize the workflow of the industry or have to upgrade or reconfigure the existing facility. They mostly rely on the CAD systems to design the new facility and equipment.
  • Industrial engineers use computer modeling/simulation for analysis and evaluation of the system and maximize efficiency.
  • The goal of industrial engineers is to reduce the time taken by the workers to perform a certain job and redistribute the work.
  • Industrial engineers are responsible for increasing productivity through proper management and efficient methods of organizing a business. Also, their role is to check and control the quality of the material used.
  • Industrial engineers assist in financial planning, production planning, cost analysis, and control management for the physical distribution of services and goods.
Future Scope Of Industrial Engineering

The job responsibilities of the industrial engineer revolve around the effective use of materials, resources, energy, information, manpower, machines, and capital. Industrial engineers can work with almost all industries in the private and public sectors.
Some of the prospective careers for industrial engineers are

  • Business systems analyst
  • Logistic manager
  • Quality control manager
  • facilities engineer
  • Human resource manager
  • Business process engineer
  • Plant supervisor
  • Production, process, assets, or manufacturing engineer
  • Scheduling and planning manager
  • Management consultant
  • Health and safety manager
  • Operations manager

Some of the sectors where industrial engineers are in demand include

  • Service industries
  • Logistics providers
  • Wholesale trade
  • Consulting and engineering services
  • Manufacturing industries
  • Research and development firms
  • Private consultancy firms
  • R&D establishments of big companies
Why Pursue A Career In Industrial Engineering?

Industrial engineers are the problem-solvers of society. They use their wide-scale knowledge and thinking skills related to manufacturing technology to enhance the efficiency of the organization. They are expected to have an in-depth understanding of the entire project lifecycle.
Here are some of the reasons why one should consider their future as an industrial engineer.

  • Due to the broad-based nature of the course, industrial engineers can work with every sector and all types of manufacturing industries. They can even work in both managerial and technical positions.
  • Industrial engineers are a part of the dynamic world with evolving technology. They have to keep up with the pace of rising technologies to bring about a revolution with innovative techniques.
  • You can customize your industrial engineering course to match your interest.
  • Industrial engineering is more focused on the processes and ways to enhance these processes i.e. it is less technical due to a small percentage of coding involved.
