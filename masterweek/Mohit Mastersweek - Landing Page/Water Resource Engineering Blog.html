Water Resource Engineering- Here’s Why It Is Important

Water is the necessity of life and is one of the most essential components for our survival. Due to the fast-growing uncertainties related to global climate change, the world is facing issues for the sustainable allocation and usage of water.

It is likely that the rising demands of water and uncontrolled water management will lead to situations that we have never encountered. As a result, water resource management practices are adopted to avoid setbacks that might arise in the allocation of water resources.
This situation gave rise to the concept of water resource engineering. This field of engineering focuses on the striving demands of water and its allocation on an equitable basis to satisfy all the uses and demands.

What Is Water Resource Engineering?

Water resource engineering is concerned with the sustainability of water-based resources. As the water is becoming scarce, the importance of water resource management is growing to a great level. There is the need to find a balance between the needs of human beings and water sustainability in the environment.
In simple definition, Water resource engineering is the leading research field of civil engineering. It addresses the projects related to flood flow management, hydrology, ground water, river flow, underground wells, natural springs, etc.
This segment of engineering is concerned with the flow of water and correlates with the design of canals, channels, dams, bridges, etc.
Moreover, it aligns with the concept of fluid mechanism and its principles to deal with the problems related to storage, collection, control, transportation, use of water, and regulation of water.

Why Is Water Resource Engineering Important?

Resources, as we know, are finite. There are many resources that are renewable such as solar, hydro, wind, and biomass. But let’s accept the fact that water being a renewable source is not as abundant as once it was, due to uncertain climate change.
Also, we are well aware of the quantity of water available to us for our usability, irrespective of its vastness. Infact, as per the facts only 3% of water available is fresh water and can be used by the humans for satisfying their needs.
Water resource engineering is therefore important to preserve this limited quantity of fresh water and understanding what works and what will not. It also develops new systems and processes that involve different analyses related to the water cycle and flow of water for sustainable water resource management.

What Does A Water Resource Engineer Do?

Water resource engineers play an essential role in the preservation and conservation of the earth’s most plentiful resource. A huge percentage of the work of water resource engineers involve management of water resources to ensure the adequate water supply. Also, they are tasked with the treatment of water to improve the quality of water at various ends be it recreational, industrial, or commercial.
Water resource engineers must therefore design new systems and equipment to enhance the efficiency of water treatment. Other tasks include data analysis from the defined areas, improving the facilities to upgrade the cleaning process for treatment of water.
Moreover, a water resource engineer at the end must look after the construction of these improved systems and its implementation to ensure that they are properly assembled. Once completed, their responsibility is to manage and oversee the maintenance of the system.

Job Responsibilities Of A Water Resource Engineer

A water resource engineer may either be employed by the state or central government or may be hired by any private organization to plan the use of water and its treatment.
They usually work on designing the pump systems, treatment plants, and pipelines for handling the waste water. They also get indulged and focus on the flood control projects and mapping of the drainage systems to manage the flow of water.
Also, they have their hands on the management of fresh water supply systems to ensure the integrated management of water that balances between the needs of humans and that of nature.
